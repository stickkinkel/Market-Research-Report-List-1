<p><p>The blister tooling market serves various applications, primarily in the thermoform and cold-form sectors. Thermoforming involves heating a plastic sheet and forming it over a mold to create protective packaging, often used for food and pharmaceuticals. Cold forming, on the other hand, shapes materials at room temperature, commonly employed in producing blister packs for tablets and capsules. Both methods enhance product safety, extend shelf life, and improve consumer convenience, making blister tooling essential in packaging industries.</p></p>
