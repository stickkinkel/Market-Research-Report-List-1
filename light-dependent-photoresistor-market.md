<p><ul><li>Intrinsic Photoresistor</li><li>Extrinsic Photoresistor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The light-dependent photoresistor market can be categorized into intrinsic and extrinsic types. Intrinsic photoresistors utilize pure semiconductor materials that exhibit significant resistance changes when exposed to light. They generally offer high sensitivity and faster response times. In contrast, extrinsic photoresistors, often made from doped semiconductors or materials with impurities, allow for more tailored sensitivity and performance characteristics. Both types find applications in various fields, including automation, telecommunications, and consumer electronics, driven by the increasing demand for smart devices.</p></p>
