<p><p>The cameras for microscopes market is predominantly categorized into CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) types. CCD cameras excel in image quality and low-light sensitivity, making them suitable for high-resolution imaging applications. In contrast, CMOS cameras offer faster frame rates and lower power consumption, making them ideal for real-time imaging and cost-sensitive applications. Both technologies cater to varied scientific and industrial demands, driving advancements in microscopy across research, medical, and manufacturing fields.</p></p>
