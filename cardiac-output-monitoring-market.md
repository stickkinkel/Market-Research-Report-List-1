<p><ul><li>Invasive Devices</li><li>Minimally-invasive Devices</li><li>Non-invasive Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cardiac output monitoring encompasses three main market types: invasive, minimally-invasive, and non-invasive devices. </p><p>Invasive devices involve direct measurement through arterial or venous catheters, providing precise data but requiring surgical procedures. Minimally-invasive devices strike a balance by using catheter technology that reduces trauma while still providing accurate readings. Non-invasive devices utilize external sensors or techniques like echocardiography to estimate cardiac output without penetrating the body. Each type serves distinct clinical needs and patient conditions, influencing the choice based on accuracy, risk, and patient comfort.</p></p>
